Step Two.

Work on that Emergency Fund

No one knows what will happen tomorrow. This may sound impractical if you are in college, but I promise a little goes a log way. Start saving up now for a things that may go wrong tomorrow. Here is a list of emergencies that may require you to break into that fund.

Step Three.

Evaluate your Debt
  1. Look at High Interest Debt (what gets charged the most by you not paying it)
    • Credit Card Debt
    • Payday Loans
  2. Look at Debt that you have the most to pay back
    • Student Loans
    • Car Loans
  3. All The Rest
    • Borrowed Money from family
    • Non-interest loans

Step Four.

Get a Credit Card

Credit cards?? Isn't that what we should stay away from? Well, not really. They are given a bad rap. The key is not to spend more money than you actually have. If you have a budget of $500 a month, don't spend more than that.
